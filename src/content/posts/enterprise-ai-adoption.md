Most of my consulting work sits between AI delivery and product strategy. I help enterprise teams turn broad goals into clearer product direction: which problems are worth solving, what data or workflow constraints matter, what a first version could look like, and what needs to be true for the work to land with real users.

That work usually involves a mix of stakeholder conversations, use case shaping, product requirements, technical architecture discussions, and deployment. I have been closest to efforts around AI and data strategy, workflow redesign, product vision, technical requirements, and tackling the challenges that come with enterprise product development.

The throughline is translation. Executives need a clear view of value and sequencing. Product and business teams need a practical version of the workflow. Engineering teams need requirements that are specific enough to build from. Good AI work depends on all of those groups sharing enough context to make better decisions.

I try to bring a technical lens to that process without losing the business reality. A use case is not mature just because it uses a model. It needs a user, a decision point, a data path, a feedback loop, and a way to tell whether it is actually improving the work.
